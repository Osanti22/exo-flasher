Exoskeleton Firmware Flasher

Flash an exoskeleton unit straight from your browser — no downloads, no command line. Plug in over USB, pick a version, and click one button.

Flash a unit

This browser can't flash. WebSerial isn't available here. Please open this page in Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge on a desktop computer (see requirements below).
WebSerial is blocked. This page must be served over https:// (GitHub Pages is fine). If you opened the file directly from disk, that won't work.

Calibration is preserved — flashing does not erase the unit's per-unit settings stored in flash.

Before you start — please read

  • Use the right browser. Chrome Edge on a desktop/laptop — or Chrome 148+ on Android with a USB-C OTG cable.
    Safari iPhone / iPad Firefox will not work.
  • Use a DATA USB cable — not a charge-only cable. Many cheap cables only carry power and the unit will never appear. Plug the exoskeleton unit into this computer.
  • Then click Connect & Flash, choose the serial port in the browser's pop-up prompt, and wait about 30–60 seconds. Don't unplug it while it's flashing.

How to confirm it worked

When flashing finishes, click “Logs & Console” in the dialog and watch the boot messages. You've succeeded if you see either of these lines: 

IMU LH up (V3.1 single: cs=17, rst/wake0 via PCA9534@0x20, no-INT, accel+gyro+rotvec)
IMU LH up (Version#1 single: cs=17, rst=8 wake=18 direct-GPIO, no-INT, accel+gyro+rotvec)

You'll only ever see one of them — the firmware auto-detects which board revision it's running on (V3.1 or Version #1) and configures the IMU accordingly. The same image works on both board revisions, so you don't need to know which one you have.

Troubleshooting

  • The port doesn't appear in the pop-up? Try a different USB cable (must be a data cable) and a different USB port.
  • The unit disconnects and reconnects on its own? A factory-blank board can briefly drop off and rejoin the USB bus — that's normal. Just try Connect & Flash again.
  • A badly-flashed unit won't connect at all? Hold the BOOT button on the unit while you plug it in (keep it held until the port appears), then release and click Connect & Flash.
